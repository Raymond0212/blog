## Executive Summary

This reporting period was dominated by agentic AI moving from experimentation into managed enterprise deployment. Anthropic and OpenAI both shipped agent platforms, coding-agent workflows, managed agents, enterprise connectors, deployment services, and governance controls intended to make autonomous tool use usable inside regulated organizations.

**Key updates:**

- Anthropic expanded Claude Code, Claude Managed Agents, Claude Cowork, Microsoft 365 integrations, legal and financial services deployments, and security/compliance integrations.
- OpenAI launched GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.5 Instant updates, expanded Codex across desktop, mobile, enterprise, AWS, Dell, and deployment-company channels, and introduced new domain offerings for clinicians, finance, cyber defense, life sciences, and voice APIs.
- Both companies made security and safety a product-level theme, with sandboxing, compliance APIs, provenance, child/youth safety, account hardening, supply-chain incident disclosures, and specialized cyber access programs.
- Anthropic introduced major Managed Agents capabilities, including self-hosted sandboxes, MCP tunnels, memory, dreaming, outcomes, webhooks, and multiagent orchestration.
- OpenAI broadened Codex from a coding assistant into a general work agent with mobile review, browser workflows, background computer use, plugins, SSH/devbox support, enterprise sandboxing, and workspace agents.
- OpenAI and Anthropic both deepened cloud and enterprise distribution through AWS, Dell, Microsoft 365, legal/financial services playbooks, global systems integrators, and dedicated deployment support.
- OpenAI released multiple safety and policy artifacts, including GPT-5.5 system cards, a GPT-5.5 Instant system card, youth and child safety blueprints, the Safety Fellowship, and Trusted Contact.
- Anthropic published extensive Claude Code operating guidance covering large codebases, subagents, 1M context, prompt caching, computer/browser use, tool design, and multi-agent coordination.

**Key trends:**

- Agent platforms are becoming operational infrastructure, with memory, orchestration, sandboxes, MCP/connectors, audit logs, and managed deployment services.
- Coding agents are broadening into general work agents that can operate across browsers, terminals, office files, cloud environments, mobile review flows, and enterprise systems.
- AI safety is shifting toward layered governance: model system cards, account controls, provenance, telemetry, trusted access, youth safeguards, and enterprise compliance integrations.
- Enterprise adoption is moving from pilots to deployment playbooks, with both vendors emphasizing governance, budget controls, observability, identity, compliance, and vertical-specific rollout patterns.
- MCP and connectors are becoming a common integration layer for letting agents reach production systems, SaaS tools, office documents, legal platforms, financial workflows, and consumer services.
- Security teams are both a target customer and a control point: vendors are shipping cyber-defense products while also documenting supply-chain incidents, sandbox controls, account hardening, and abuse-prevention models.
- Frontier model releases are increasingly paired with public safety documentation, specialized access programs, and red-team or bug-bounty structures rather than simple capability announcements.
- Domain-specific AI is accelerating in regulated sectors, especially legal, finance, healthcare, life sciences, cyber defense, and education.
