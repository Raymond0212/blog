### [Introducing GeneBench-Pro](https://openai.com/index/introducing-genebench-pro/)
**Published:** 2026-06-30

OpenAI introduced GeneBench-Pro, a research-level benchmark for evaluating whether AI agents can perform ambiguity-heavy, end-to-end computational biology analysis rather than just execute narrow predefined workflows. The benchmark contains 129 synthetic but realistic problems across 10 biology domains, designed to test higher-order judgment such as choosing valid analysis paths, revising assumptions, and deciding when evidence is decision-ready. OpenAI reports its top model, GPT-5.6 Sol, achieving a 28.7% pass rate at the highest reasoning level, rising to 31.5% with Pro mode, which it presents as evidence of rapid progress in scientific reasoning while still short of expert-level reliability.

### [Core dump epidemiology: fixing an 18-year-old bug](https://openai.com/index/core-dump-epidemiology-data-infrastructure-bug/)
**Published:** 2026-06-30

OpenAI describes how it investigated seemingly impossible C++ crashes in Rockset, a key part of its ChatGPT data infrastructure, by shifting from manual core-dump inspection to a population-level analysis pipeline over a year of production crashes. That analysis revealed two unrelated causes that had been conflated: silent hardware corruption on a single Azure host and an 18-year-old race condition in GNU `libunwind` that surfaced during exception unwinding. OpenAI says the result was both an immediate operational fix, such as denylisting the bad host and improving crash diagnostics, and a broader lesson that epidemiology-style debugging can expose patterns that single-case analysis misses.
