### [Claude Code now supports artifacts](https://claude.com/blog/artifacts-in-claude-code)
**Published:** 2026-06-18

Anthropic announced that Claude Code can now publish artifacts: live, interactive web pages generated from the full context of a coding session, including code, connected tools, and the conversation. The feature is aimed at collaboration use cases such as PR walkthroughs, incident timelines, dashboards, and release checklists, with private sharing inside Team and Enterprise organizations.

It also expanded spend controls with default workspace limits, group-level limits, and user-specific overrides, making enterprise rollout and budget governance more manageable.

### [Improving health intelligence in ChatGPT](https://openai.com/index/improving-health-intelligence-in-chatgpt/)
**Published:** 2026-06-18

OpenAI says GPT-5.5 Instant materially improves ChatGPT’s handling of health questions, especially around urgent-care recognition, context gathering, uncertainty handling, and clarity. The article ties these gains to both model improvements and a physician-led evaluation pipeline using HealthBench and HealthBench Professional, with the improvements now available to free users.

---

### [Using AI to help physicians diagnose rare genetic diseases affecting children](https://openai.com/index/diagnose-rare-childhood-diseases/)
**Published:** 2026-06-18

OpenAI says researchers from Boston Children’s Hospital, Harvard, and OpenAI used `o3 Deep Research` to reanalyze 376 previously unsolved rare-disease cases using de-identified clinical and genomic data. After expert review and confirmatory testing, physicians established 18 diagnoses, with the system framed as a hypothesis generator for clinicians rather than an autonomous diagnostic tool.

---

### [A near-autonomous AI chemist improves a challenging reaction in medicinal chemistry](https://openai.com/index/ai-chemist-improves-reaction/)
**Published:** 2026-06-17

OpenAI reports that GPT-5.4, paired with Molecule.one’s Maria AI and an automated lab workflow, identified a reaction setup that improved a difficult medicinal-chemistry coupling. The result is presented as near-autonomous rather than fully autonomous, with human chemists still selecting proposals, correcting plans, and validating outcomes.

---

### [Introducing LifeSciBench](https://openai.com/index/introducing-life-sci-bench/)
**Published:** 2026-06-17

OpenAI introduced LifeSciBench, a benchmark intended to test whether AI systems can handle realistic life-science research tasks instead of narrow biology QA. The benchmark includes 750 expert-authored tasks across multiple workflows and biological domains, and OpenAI says current systems are still far from saturating performance on the harder artifact-heavy tasks.

---

### [Predicting model behavior before release by simulating deployment](https://openai.com/index/deployment-simulation/)
**Published:** 2026-06-16

OpenAI described Deployment Simulation, a pre-release safety method that replays de-identified real conversation contexts with candidate models to estimate how often undesired behaviors may appear after launch. The company says the method improved prediction of post-release misbehavior rates, reduced test-awareness effects, and surfaced at least one novel failure mode before deployment.

### [Introducing the OpenAI Partner Network](https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-partner-network/)
**Published:** 2026-06-14

The partner-network launch is also an engineering-enablement move: OpenAI is building a services channel around strategy, integration, workflow redesign, and adoption support for enterprise deployments. The program is designed to help organizations move from experimentation to production implementation with certified external help.

### [Predicting model behavior before release by simulating deployment](https://openai.com/index/deployment-simulation/)
**Published:** 2026-06-16

Deployment Simulation is explicitly a safety technique: it uses realistic deployment-like prompts to detect undesirable behavior before release instead of relying only on standard evaluations. The approach is notable because it aims to find risk signals in conditions closer to actual usage and could extend to agentic tool-use settings and external audits.
