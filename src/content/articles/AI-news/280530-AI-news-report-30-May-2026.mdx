## Executive Summary

This week centered on production-grade agent systems, with Anthropic and OpenAI both emphasizing orchestration, eval-driven improvement, and stronger governance around higher-risk deployments. The biggest practical shift is that agentic workflows are being packaged as enterprise-operable products rather than experimental demos.

**Key updates:**

- Anthropic introduced Claude Code dynamic workflows and highlighted enterprise deployment patterns around self-hosted sandboxes, MCP tunnels, agent orchestration, and secure code review.
- OpenAI published new governance and evaluation guidance while expanding trusted biodefense access and showing how Codex can drive self-improving production agents.
- Security moved closer to the product core, with Zero Trust guidance for AI agents, LLM-assisted source-code defense workflows, and public-facing governance controls for frontier models.

**Key trends:**

- Agent systems are shifting from single-assistant usage toward orchestrated multi-agent workflows with verification loops, evals, and bounded autonomy.
- Governance is becoming more concrete, with public frameworks, third-party evaluation methodology, and access controls tied to sensitive domains like biology and frontier-model safety.
- Vendors are increasingly framing AI adoption as an operational design problem that spans engineering, security, compliance, and business process ownership.
