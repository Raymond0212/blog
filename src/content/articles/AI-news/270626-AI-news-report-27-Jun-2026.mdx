This week’s updates were dominated by AI moving deeper into operational systems: agentic work models, enterprise rollout, security workflows, and infrastructure specialization all advanced at once. Anthropic focused on how organizations should structure agent access and collaboration, while OpenAI combined flagship model news with a broad set of enterprise, security, standards, and deployment case studies.

**Key updates:**

- OpenAI previewed GPT-5.6 Sol with an explicitly tighter safety posture around cyber and bio misuse, alongside lower-cost Terra and faster Luna variants.
- OpenAI expanded its security push with Daybreak, Patch the Planet, and standards work around third-party evaluation and conformity criteria.

**Key trends:**

- AI vendors are treating agents less as standalone assistants and more as managed coworkers that need identity, memory, oversight, and role boundaries.
- Enterprise adoption stories are shifting from experimentation narratives to measurable workflow redesign, deployment scale, and governance controls.
- Security and safety work is becoming more operational, spanning misuse controls, defender tooling, open-source patching, and cross-industry standards.
- Model vendors are increasingly pairing software launches with infrastructure, ecosystem, and public-policy positioning rather than shipping models in isolation.
